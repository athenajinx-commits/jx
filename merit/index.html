The Context

The world is changing
faster than its people.

Automation and artificial intelligence are not just disrupting industries — they are dismantling the social structures that gave people identity, community, and purpose. A job was never just a paycheck. It was routine, belonging, meaning. When the job disappears, what replaces it?

Universal Basic Income may cover survival. But survival is not the same as thriving. People need structure, challenge, recognition, and community. MERIT is designed to provide all of that — not as charity, but as a system where positive contribution earns real rewards.

What Is MERIT?

One place.
Five functions.

MERIT is not a gym, a hotel, a school, a spa, or a community centre — it is all of them at once, unified by a single credit economy. Every positive action you take within the MERIT ecosystem earns you credits. Those credits reduce what you pay, unlock premium access, and reflect your standing in the community.

The Engine

A credit system
built on merit.

MERIT Credits (MC) are earned by doing positive things and spent on access, upgrades, and privileges within the community. The system is transparent, community-governed, and designed to feel like opportunity — not surveillance.

The Architecture

A building designed
for human thriving.

Every zone in a MERIT facility is designed to encourage positive engagement. The space is not a passive amenity — it is an active ecosystem where movement through the building itself creates opportunities to earn, learn, and connect.

01 — Ground Floor
The Commons
Open-plan social areas, café, co-working desks, notice boards, and community events space. The heartbeat of MERIT — always activated, never empty.
◆ Social events earn +150 MC per participation
02 — Level 2–3
The Movement Hall
Full gym floor, group fitness studios, climbing wall, swimming pool. All tracked via wearable integration. Every session logged and credited automatically.
◆ Physical activity earns up to +200 MC/session
03 — Level 4
The Learning Quarter
Classrooms, maker spaces, recording studios, and a library. Skills taught here — from cooking to coding to carpentry — are the highest-earning credit activities.
◆ Teaching earns 5× more than attending
04 — Level 5
The Sanctuary
Spa, sauna, flotation tanks, therapy rooms, quiet meditation spaces. Mental wellness is credited equally to physical. Therapist access is part of every tier.
◆ Mindfulness streaks earn +75 MC/day
05 — Levels 6–10
Residences
Private rooms graded by tier. From compact, beautifully designed seedling rooms to expansive steward suites. All include natural light, sound insulation, and a desk.
◆ Room quality scales with monthly MC average
06 — Rooftop
The Garden
Community kitchen garden, outdoor fitness, event lawn, and sky terrace. Tending the garden earns credits. Hosting a rooftop event earns more.
◆ Garden stewardship earns +250 MC/shift
The Guardrails

This is not
Black Mirror.

The inspiration came from the episode. The warning came from the episode too. A merit system that becomes coercive, performative, or punitive is a dystopia. MERIT is designed with hard ethical boundaries from the ground up — not as an afterthought, but as the foundation.

01
Voluntary Always
Participation in the credit system is always opt-in. Basic housing and meals are never withheld based on credit score. Floor-level access is guaranteed for all residents.
02
Transparent Rules
Every rule is publicly posted and community-voted. No hidden scoring. No algorithmic black boxes. You always know exactly why your score changed.
03
Democratic Governance
The credit rules are voted on by the community, not set by a corporation. Steward and Elder tier members hold seats on the rules council.
04
No Surveillance
Credits are earned by participation, not by monitoring. No cameras track your behaviour. No AI judges your mood. You choose what you log.
05
Lucky Chance
Weekly lotteries mean that effort and luck both play a role — exactly like life. You don't have to be a high-performer to experience windfall moments.
06
Credits Cannot Be Bought
Wealth cannot buy Merit Credits. The only way to earn them is through action. This levels the playing field between UBI recipients and higher earners.

Getting There

From proposal
to prototype.

MERIT begins as a single pilot building in one city — and grows from there as the model is refined, trusted, and replicated.

Phase 01 — Now
Research & Proposal
Stakeholder consultation, academic partnerships, policy engagement. Building the evidence base and finding co-founders.
Phase 02 — Year 1–2
Pilot Facility
A single 150-resident building in a mid-sized city. Test the credit system, gather data, iterate on what works. Partner with a UBI trial cohort.
Phase 03 — Year 3–5
Network Expansion
Replicate the model in 5 cities. Credits become portable — earn in Toronto, spend in Amsterdam. Build the governance charter.
Phase 04 — Year 5+
Open Standard
Publish MERIT as an open-source framework. Any city, housing provider, or government can build a MERIT facility. The model belongs to everyone.
